"Some Details"

When I move to a new apartment, the first thing I do is build shelves. When I get to a hotel, I unpack before I do anything else. When I move to a new office, I slowly fill my cube walls with pictures, sketches, whiteboards, cards, doodads, and little fake birds.

At my work desk I have 2 small desk lamps and a string of tiny lights. I have a collection of warm and cool grey artist markers. I have several weights of black pens. I have a copy of Bringhurst, of Tufte, and most of the Book Apart books, marked with tape flags. I have a huge pair of headphones and a teacup shaped like an owl.

I wear all black, almost always. Sometimes I wear a little bit of red, sometimes a white shirt. Once a year I go to the beach and wear all color and no black. The only thing that varies in color throughout the year is my hair.

My clothes are organized by item, then by color. My books are shelved by subject, then size (except for the books that I reread, which are all together in my nightstand).

I have collections of: records, teapots, rubber stamps, occult books, the works of Mike Mignola, and art books on John Singer Sargent. I like reading books backwards. I like taking a record out of its sleeve and putting it on a turntable.

I like drawing my own comics with ink and brushes and pens and have no desire to draw digitally. I like my collection of printed comics.

I do not wish to get Lasik surgery, because I like my glasses, and I like how I see when I take them off.
